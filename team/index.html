Barry Uphoff – Managing Partner

Barry Uphoff is the Managing Partner of CHSO. Barry is a Partner at Capricorn Investment Group. Prior to joining Capricorn, Barry was a senior investment professional at Silver Point Capital. Prior to Silver Point, Barry was a Director with Trivest, where he led the firm’s healthcare investing activities. Prior to Trivest, Barry was a founding Partner of Diamond Management Consultants, where he led the firm’s global healthcare and strategy practices.

Barry earned his BA from the Johns Hopkins University, Phi Beta Kappa. Barry read for a Diploma in Law and an MSc in Medicine at Oxford University as a Rhodes Scholar. Barry also received an MBA from the University of Chicago.

Shahab Vagefi – Partner

Shahab Vagefi is a Partner at CHSO. Prior to joining CHSO, Shahab was a member of the investment team at Capricorn Investment Group. Prior to Capricorn, Shahab was an investment professional in the U.S. Growth Buyout fund at The Carlyle Group – focused on middle-market buyout and growth equity investments primarily in the technology, media and telecommunications; business services; and education sectors. Previously, Shahab worked in investment banking with Credit Suisse First Boston and JP Morgan H&Q.

Shahab earned a joint BA-MA degree with Phi Beta Kappa honors in International Relations and International Economics at the Johns Hopkins University and the Paul H. Nitze School of Advanced International Studies (SAIS). He obtained an MBA from the Kellogg School of Management at Northwestern University.

Michael McCullough, MD – Partner, Senior Clinical Advisor

Michael McCullough, MD is a Partner and Senior Clinical Advisor at CHSO. Michael was previously a Partner at Headwaters Capital Partners and a healthcare advisor to Venrock, NanoDimension, 2U, and Vocera. Michael was Founder of QuestBridge, BeAGoodDoctor.Org and RegenMed Systems. Michael advises multiple tech oriented funds on medical investments including Greylock Partners and Redpoint Ventures. Michael was elected a Kauffman Fellow in 2009 and an Ashoka Fellow in 2004.

Michael earned his BA from Stanford University, Phi Beta Kappa, in Human Biology. Michael obtained a M.Sc. in Diagnostic Imaging from Oxford University as a Rhodes Scholar and read for a M.A. in Philosophy, Politics and Economics. Michael obtained his MD from UCSF and Emergency Medicine board certification from Stanford. Michael is a part-time Assistant Professor of Emergency Medicine at Stanford and UCSF affiliated trauma centers. He serves on the board of the Dalai Lama Foundation.

Aabed Meer, MD – Director

Aabed Meer, MD is a Director at CHSO. He has also been an Investment Associate at Capricorn Investment Group focused on healthcare investments. Prior to Capricorn, Aabed spent time with New Enterprise Associates, as well as the medical device incubator, ExploraMed Development. He has researched health economics issues domestically and abroad with Kaiser Permanente and the UK’s National Health Service.

Aabed received an undergraduate degree in Biochemical Sciences from Harvard University and an MBA from Stanford University, where he also attended medical school and served on a committee of the university’s Board of Trustees. He is a published author and his work has been cited by The Wall Street Journal, BusinessWeek and ABC News.

Mario Moreno – Director

Mario Moreno is a Director at CHSO. Prior to joining CHSO, Mario worked as an Associate in the Buyout fund at The Carlyle Group focused on equity investments in the healthcare sector. Prior to Carlyle, Mario worked in investment banking with Credit Suisse focused on M&A, leveraged finance, equity and debt capital market transactions primarily in the Retail & Consumer and Latin America groups.

He earned his bachelor of business administration from the Goizueta Business School at Emory University with a double major in Finance and International Business. He obtained an MBA from the Wharton School of Business at the University of Pennsylvania.

Vladimir Andonov – Senior Associate

Vladimir Andonov is a Senior Associate at CHSO. Prior to CHSO, Vladimir worked at Capricorn Investment Group as an Investment Analyst. Prior to Capricorn, Vladimir was an investment banking Analyst at J.P. Morgan in New York and London in the Financial Institutions group.

Vladimir received a BA in Economics and Political Science from Williams College. He obtained an MBA from the University of Chicago Booth School of Business and is a CFA charterholder.

Chris Beall – Senior Associate

Chris Beall is a Senior Associate at CHSO. Prior to CHSO, Chris worked at Quad-C Management Inc. as an Associate focused on middle market buyouts. Prior to Quad-C, Chris worked as an investment banking Analyst in the Retail & Consumer group at Citi.

He received a BS in Commerce from the McIntire School of Commerce at the University of Virginia.

Calvin Nguyen – Associate

Calvin Nguyen is an Associate at CHSO. Prior to joining CHSO, Calvin was an Associate focused on investments in the healthcare sector at Genstar Capital in San Francisco. Prior to Genstar, Calvin was an Analyst in the General Banking group at Lazard Frères & Co. in New York, where he focused on mergers and acquisitions in the Industrial, Automotive, and Retail & Consumer sectors.

He holds a bachelor of business administration in Finance, with honors, from the Goizueta Business School at Emory University.

Phillip Campbell – Associate

Phillip Campbell is an Associate at CHSO. Prior to joining CHSO, Phillip was an Associate at CIT Group in New York City, where he focused on leverage finance and M&A advisory services in healthcare. Prior to CIT, Phillip was an Analyst at HJ Sims, a merchant bank exclusively operating in the senior living space.

Phillip holds a bachelor of science in Finance & Accounting from the Carroll School of Management at Boston College.

Galym Imanbayev – Associate

Galym Imanbayev is an Associate at CHSO. Prior to CHSO, Galym worked as a Management Consulting Associate at Pricewaterhouse Coopers, LLP in the health industries group.

Galym received a BA in Economics from Stanford University. While at Stanford, he researched novel treatments in regenerative medicine in the Departments of Orthopedic Surgery and Bioengineering, and has published in the Journal of the American College of Cardiology, Stem Cell & Development, and Acta Biomaterialia.
